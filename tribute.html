Dr. Neil DeGrasse Tyson

The science communicator of our time

Here's a timeline of Dr. Tyson's life:

  • 1958 - Born in Manhattan, New York City.
  • 1973 - From kindergarten throughout high school, Tyson attended public schools in the Bronx. He also attended astronomy courses offered by the Hayden Planetarium
  • 1975 - Famous astronomer Carl Sagan, who was a faculty member at Cornell University, tried to recruit Tyson to Cornell for undergraduate studies
  • 1976 - He attended The Bronx High School of Science where he was captain of the wrestling team, editor-in-chief of the Physical Science Journal, and graduated.
  • 1977 - Tyson chose to attend Harvard where he majored in physics and lived in Currier House.
  • 1980 - Tyson earned a degree in physics at Harvard College.
  • 1983 - He then began his graduate work at the University of Texas at Austin, from which he received an masters degree in astronomy.
  • 1986 - Tyson was a lecturer in astronomy at the University of Maryland untill 1987.
  • 1988 - He was accepted into the astronomy graduate program at Columbia University, where he earned an MPhil degree in astrophysics in 1989, and a PhD degree in astrophysics in 1991
  • 1991 - In the course of his thesis work, he went to Chile, where he obtained images for the Cal√°n/Tololo Supernova Survey helping to further their work in establishing Type Ia supernovae as standard candles.
  • 1994 - Tyson joined the Hayden Planetarium as a staff scientist while he was a research affiliate in Princeton University.
  • 1996 - He became acting director of the planetarium in June 1995 and was appointed director in 1996.
  • 2001 - U.S. President George W. Bush appointed Tyson to serve on the Commission on the Future of the United States Aerospace Industry and in 2004 to serve on the "Moon, Mars, and Beyond" commission.
  • 2004 and beyond - He wrote many books and is seen today as the Greatest Science Comunicator of our time.
